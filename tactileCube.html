Heading

In the Spring of 2019, I worked with a team at Darthmouth's Applied Learning and Innovation Lab (DALI Lab) to develop an educational toy for children with autism.Inspired by the ways that texture, color, and dynamic components can engage severely impacted children, we worked with educators and their students to develop the TactTiles Box. The system includes a 8x8 box and magnetized tiles that can be easily swapped and stored within.

Process

This project stems from Carson Levine and and Izzy Parizeau's coursework in impactful design, Ryleigh's Box. The success of the initial prototype caught the attention of Dartmouth's Magnussen Center for Entrepreneurship and DALI Lab. Our goal was to take Ryleigh's Box and make it better.

What is better?

Feedback from Mr. Ryleigh's caretaker and teachers suggested that Carson and Izzy had found a powerful solution for an underserved community. Working with teachers and occupational therapists from the Hartford Autism Regional Program (HARP), we defined the following metrics for improvement and success:

Adaptability
Portability

Our user's needs and interests are highly individual-specific. The TactTiles box addresses the need for adaptability through its interchangeable magnetized sides. Educators and therapists can swap tiles to meet the varying goals and interests of each user session.

Educators and therapists often work in multi-purpose spaces that constantly need to be rearranged. Early protoypes of Ryleigh's box were inconvenient to move. We reduced the size of the box, designed the tiles to stow away inside, and added a simple, low-profile carry strap that makes it easy to carry, take out, and put away.

Expanded Use Case

Ryleigh's Box was designed to stimulate and engage Mr. Ryleigh. With the TactTiles Box we hope to leverage this engagement for education and mood regulation.

Quick Production

Bespoke sensory solutions are available on Etsy- however we want to create a quality product that can be built quickly and consistently.
