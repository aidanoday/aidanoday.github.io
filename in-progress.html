Twabler
Machine learning and NLP have a lot of potential to help researchers and other groups gather information from open forums like twitter. But some things are confusing for machines. For example: If we want to teach machines how to differentiate between muni lines and street names, we can use humans to quickly label trainer tweets.

I’ve been working with the Code for SF brigade to create a clean, fast, efficient UI for an app that can let researchers and volunteers churn through tweets.

I love this project because the team is great, and because it has taught me a lot about marrying front and backend, and challenged me to think of ways to make something like data labeling snappy and fun!
Rent Cap
I’ve been working with another team at Code for SF to create a tool to help tennants understand if they are covered by new rent protections put in place by the Tennant Protection Act of 2019.

Using a flow-style survey, we use simple questions to identify if tennants are protected- or to direct them to the relevant resources if they are not.

We wanted to make the site feel warm, official, and informative- the yellow and black scheme gives it the feel of a call to action aswell!

Quick Builds

t:kr
This fall I was living in San Francisco and taking care of my 5 year old cousin, C. C can read numbers, but has a really hard time understanding digital clocks. For him, even the difference between 3:42 and 3:43 is practically meanigless.

I came up with t:kr (ticker) to help him translate digital clock displays and learn about the temporal distance.

Set up on wall-mounted rollers, this is the second prototype. It features a full 12-hour day.

Reframing Canada Goose
The inspiration for this project came from a course that I took: ENGS 012: Design Thinking. The assignment was to reframe a common idea, and communicate that reframe using Photoshop to design a poster.

Canada Goose jackets are controversial- most justify their purchase by saying: “Yes, it is expensive, but it’s also very warm. It’s worth the cost.” Canada Goose itself often puts out material claiming that their jackets are highly technical and flaunting purported quality certifications.

I want to challenge that justification by highlighting some of the non-monetary costs of Canada Goose jackets. A poster like this aims to change how the audience sees Canada Goose jackets.

Is a Canada Goose Jacket really neccessary in New England? Possibly. But this poster says that these jackets are not elegant investments in comfort; they are expensive fashion statements with additional costs to be considered.
