UX Design Background

Early Days

My design career started in early 2018. At first I was just taking design courses, developing better ways for messy students to carry things from class to class, prototyping different textures aimed at helping severely autistic students regulate energy levels, and helping school administrators understand how and why students are cheating at an Ivy League institution.

When I graduated, I volunteered at civic hack nights to help people understand rent protection elligibility and find better ways to label thousands of tweets while I was traveling in San Francisco.

In Fall 2019 I also built this site, then tried to rip out all the html and get around wix gatekeeping to build the 2019 version of this site, which was a frankenstein's monster of a site.

During quarantine, I worked with my brother, Kieran, to build a website for our pottery. I designed it and he built it. He's a very talented developer.

Founding Designer at ILWE, INC

In Nov 2019, I joined my friends PJ and Maddie in trying to improve networking in creative industries as the technical design co-founder, defacto CPO and Head of Product at ILOVEWHATEVER. We spent a year and a half building a social media site for artists that aimed to make portolio building easier.

In Aug 2020, in response to persistent user questions about an app, we spent another year developing and porting that web-platform-social-portoflio-experience into a pocket-sized, iphone-and-android-friendly app.

The whole time we had been struggling with efforts to make our tool powerful, flexible, while comprehensive while keeping it simple enought that users could onboard without too much difficulty. In the end, we decided quite recently to simplify the app and center it around creative connections, renaming it from "ILWE" to "Link" to make it easier to say.

And so, we spent the end of the summer pivoting and stripping that concept to the minimum to create a simpler, more elegant, more useful experience.
